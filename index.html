Minesweeper
is a logic-based computer game where the objective is to uncover tiles on a grid, potentially hiding mines, while avoiding detonating any of them. The rules of Minesweeper are relatively straightforward:
1. GameBoard:
Minesweeper is typically played on a square grid divided into tiles.
2. Mines:
Mines are placed on the board, and their number is predetermined before the game starts. The player's goal is to mark all the mines on the board without accidentally uncovering them.
3. Marking Tiles:
Players can mark tiles on the board as potentially containing mines using flags or other markers. This helps prevent accidental mine detonations.
4. Uncovering Tiles:
Players start by uncovering a single tile on the board. When a tile is uncovered, it reveals how many mines are adjacent to it. Based on these hints, players make decisions about their next moves.
5. Numbers on Tiles:
If a number is displayed on an uncovered tile, it indicates how many mines are adjacent to that tile. Players can use these numbers to logically deduce where mines are not present and where caution is required.
6. Winning:
Players win the game by marking all the mines on the board. Marking all the mines is the primary objective of the game.
7. Losing:
Players lose the game if they uncover a tile with a mine. In such a case, the board is revealed, and the game is lost.
Minesweeper requires logical thinking, deduction, and risk assessment. Solving more complex Minesweeper puzzles can be challenging because it involves making more advanced decisions based on the information available on the board.
